About

I am an eighth-year Ph.D. student of HiLT lab, College of Engineering, University of North Texas, supervised by Dr. Eduardo Blanco and Dr. Yan Huang. Before entering UNT, I obtained my master degree in Information Science and bachelor degree in Mathematics and Applied Mathematics, from the University of Pittsburgh and Shenzhen University, respectively.

My research interests include natural language processing and deep learning. Specifically, I am currently working on the project of mining spatio-temporal knowledge from social media. Besides the research, I am also interested in working out in gym and travelling.

Research

Spatio-Temporal Information Extraction

UNT · Aug 2019 – Feb 2023
  • Propose new tasks of determining if Twitter users are located in the mentioned location and how long they will stay at the geotagged location.
  • Create multiple corpora with quality guaranteed by filtering out bad annotators and unreliable annotations. Design interfaces to collect annotations on Amazon Mechanical Turk.
  • Build multimodal and context-aware neural networks to tackle these problems. Experimental results show that both text and image are essential, and incorporating temporal commonsense is beneficial.
